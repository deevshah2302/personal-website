Battery Pack Design for Solar EV

UBC Solar — January 2025 – March 2025

Designed and developed a 32S13P Li-ion battery pack for UBC Solar’s race vehicle, delivering a total capacity of approximately 5.25 kWh at 120 V. The pack was engineered for safety, serviceability, and manufacturability while optimizing cooling performance through CFD-based thermal modeling.

Engineering Highlights

  • 7–8 mΩ contact resistance in a non-welded configuration — comparable to spot-welded setups (~6 mΩ).
  • Refined CAD layout within a 25 × 20 × 8 inch enclosure meeting packaging & safety constraints.
  • Validated cooling airflow via Ansys & Altair CFD — improved thermal performance by 15%.
  • Automated OCV + impedance testing in Python for 700+ cells, reducing testing time by 50%.
  • Developed PCB layouts in Altium and 3D-printed fixtures for prototype assembly.

Tools Used

SolidWorks • Altium • Ansys/Altair CFD • Python • Composite Fabrication

Impact

Improved standardization for future battery testing workflows and enhanced reliability of module-level designs used by UBC Solar’s next-gen pack.
