Battery Pack Design for Solar EV

UBC Solar — January 2025 – March 2025

Overview

Designed and developed a 32S13P Li-ion battery pack for UBC Solar’s race vehicle, delivering a total capacity of approximately 5.25 kWh at 120 V. The pack was engineered for safety, serviceability, and manufacturability while optimizing cooling performance through CFD-based thermal modeling.

Engineering Highlights

  • Achieved 7–8 mΩ contact resistance in a non-welded configuration — comparable to traditional spot-welded setups (~6 mΩ).
  • Refined CAD layout to fit within a 25 × 20 × 8 inch enclosure while meeting packaging and safety constraints.
  • Validated cell spacing and airflow using Ansys & Altair CFD, improving pack thermal performance by 15 %.
  • Automated OCV + impedance testing in Python for 700 + cells, reducing testing time by 50 %.
  • Developed custom PCB layouts in Altium and 3D-printed fixtures for prototype assembly.

Tools & Techniques

SolidWorks (CAD) • Altium PCB Design • Ansys & Altair (CFD/FEA) • Python (automation scripts) • Composite testing (ASTM standards)

Impact

The pack design reduced electrical variability across cells, extended pack lifetime by ~2 years, and established a standardized workflow for cell characterization within the UBC Solar team.
